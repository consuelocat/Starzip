About

Between two worlds,
at home in all of them

Born in China, rooted in Sweden, I move through the world with a camera and an open heart. Nine years in Waldorf kindergartens taught me that childhood is sacred. Educational research showed me that learning is a form of art. Photography reminds me every day that light — like love — is everywhere.

I write. I teach. I grow organic things. I watch Ghibli films on rainy afternoons with a cat on my lap. And I'm building something beautiful.

How I see the world

Photography

A photograph is a conversation with time

I don't take photographs — I receive them. A camera is a listening device. The job of a photographer is to become very quiet, very still, so that a moment can choose to enter the frame. This is something I learned from forests first, and from children second.

The cat sits longer
than the photographer —
she knows how to wait.
Education & Research

Learning as a living art

Waldorf education gave me back my belief in slowness. In a world optimizing for speed, I spent nine years watching children knead bread, water seeds, and listen to stories by candlelight. That is my research: what happens to a child when beauty is the curriculum?

Writing & Organic Living

Words are seeds. Plants are sentences.

I write because some things cannot be photographed. The smell of rain on pine needles. The particular quiet of early morning in Göteborg. My organic shop is not a business — it is an extension of a philosophy: that what we put in and on our bodies is a form of storytelling.

Between East and West

Two cultures, one heart

Growing up between China and Sweden means I am always translating — not just language, but light. Chinese ink painting taught me negative space. Swedish seasons taught me about surviving darkness with grace. My work lives in the conversation between these two inheritances.
